PIP Statutory Interest Calculator
Florida overdue PIP benefits — Fla. Stat. §627.736(4)(d) & §55.03 · Presuit use
How to use this (click to collapse)

1. Enter the overdue benefit amount (the unpaid PIP principal you're claiming).

2. Enter the date interest begins — the date the insurer was furnished written notice of the amount of the covered loss. (PIP benefits are "overdue" 30 days after notice, but §627.736(4)(d) calculates interest from the date of that written notice.)

3. Enter the through date (today, or your projected demand/payment date).

4. Click Auto-build periods. Interest is simple (not compounded) and the §55.03 rate is set for the quarter the payment became overdue, then re-adjusts every January 1 until paid — the tool splits the timeline at each Jan 1 and fills in the correct rate automatically. You can override any rate.

5. Read the total at the bottom, or hit Print / Save as PDF to attach the breakdown to a demand.
